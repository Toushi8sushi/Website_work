Our Fall Hackathon 2024 was an incredible success! Over the weekend of October 12-13, 80+ students came together for 48 hours of innovation, collaboration, and creativity. Here's a recap of this amazing event.

## Event Overview

The Fall Hackathon brought together students from all years and majors to build innovative projects addressing real-world problems. Participants formed teams, brainstormed ideas, and coded their way through two intense days of development.

### By the Numbers

- **80+ participants** from various departments
- **22 project submissions** across multiple categories
- **15 mentors** from industry and academia
- **8 sponsors** providing resources and prizes
- **Countless cups of coffee** consumed

## Categories and Themes

Teams could choose from several categories:

**Web Applications**: Build full-stack web apps solving everyday problems
**Mobile Development**: Create Android or iOS applications
**AI/ML Projects**: Leverage artificial intelligence and machine learning
**Hardware/IoT**: Integrate hardware with software solutions
**Social Good**: Projects addressing social or environmental challenges

## Winning Projects

### First Place: EcoTrack

A mobile application that gamifies sustainable living by tracking carbon footprint and suggesting eco-friendly alternatives. The team integrated IoT sensors to monitor energy usage and built a social platform for users to share their sustainability journey.

**Team Members**: Sarah Johnson, Mike Chen, Priya Sharma, Alex Kim

### Second Place: StudyBuddy AI

An AI-powered study companion that generates personalized quizzes, summarizes lecture content, and adapts to individual learning styles. The natural language processing capabilities impressed the judges.

**Team Members**: James Wilson, Emma Davis, Ryan Patel

### Third Place: AccessiWeb

A browser extension that automatically enhances website accessibility by adjusting contrast, font sizes, and adding screen reader improvements. The project addressed an important need for inclusive web experiences.

**Team Members**: Jordan Lee, Taylor Martinez, Casey Brown, Sam Rivera

## Special Awards

**Best UI/UX**: LocalConnect - A platform connecting residents with local businesses
**Most Innovative Use of Technology**: HealthPredict - ML-based early disease detection
**Best First-Time Hackers**: QuickRecipe - Recipe discovery based on available ingredients
**People's Choice**: CampusNav - AR-based campus navigation for new students

## Highlights and Memorable Moments

### Opening Ceremony

The event kicked off Friday evening with inspiring talks from our sponsors and alumni who shared their startup journeys. The energy in the room was electric as teams formed and ideas began flowing.

### Midnight Pizza Party

No hackathon is complete without late-night fuel! The midnight pizza delivery was met with cheers and gave participants a chance to take a break, socialize, and see what other teams were building.

### Lightning Talks

Throughout the weekend, mentors gave 15-minute lightning talks on topics ranging from API design to machine learning deployment. These sessions provided valuable knowledge and inspiration for participants.

### Final Presentations

Sunday afternoon, teams showcased their projects to judges and attendees. The creativity and technical skill on display was remarkable, especially considering the short timeframe.

## Lessons Learned

Many participants shared key takeaways:

- **Time management is crucial**: Scoping projects appropriately for 48 hours
- **Communication matters**: Clear team communication prevents duplicate work
- **MVPs work**: Focus on core functionality before adding features
- **Sleep helps**: Taking short breaks improves productivity
- **Asking for help is smart**: Mentors are there to guide you
